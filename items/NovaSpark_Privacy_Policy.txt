1. Introduction and Purpose
NovaSpark Technologies Pvt. Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "NovaSpark," "the Company," "we," "us," or "our") is a technology services company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its registered office at 47/B, Koramangala 5th Block, Bengaluru - 560095, Karnataka, India. NovaSpark provides software-as-a-service (SaaS) platforms, enterprise data analytics solutions, and cloud integration services to clients across India, the European Union, Southeast Asia, and North America.
In the course of delivering our products and services, NovaSpark collects, processes, stores, and transfers personal data relating to our customers, employees, vendors, partners, and website visitors. We recognize that the protection of personal data is a fundamental right and a critical trust element of our business relationships.
This Privacy and Data Protection Policy ("the Policy") has been established to:
Define how NovaSpark collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal data
Set out the rights of individuals (Data Principals) whose data we process
Document our obligations as a Data Fiduciary under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) and as a Data Controller under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Provide a framework for accountability, governance, and compliance across all business units
Ensure transparency in our data handling practices

This Policy applies to all personal data processing activities carried out by NovaSpark, its wholly-owned subsidiaries, and its authorized data processors. It supersedes all prior versions of the company's privacy policy and is to be read in conjunction with the Data Retention Schedule (NST-SCH-DR-001), Breach Response Plan (NST-BRP-001), and Third-Party Data Processing Agreement Template (NST-TMP-DPA-001).

5. Legal Basis for Processing
NovaSpark processes personal data only where a valid legal basis exists. The applicable legal bases under the DPDP Act and GDPR are:

5.1 Consent
NovaSpark obtains free, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent from data principals before processing their personal data for purposes not strictly necessary for contractual performance. Consent is obtained through:
Affirmative tick-box mechanisms on sign-up forms and preference centers
Separate, granular consent requests for optional data uses (such as marketing communications)
Clear, plain-language consent notices available in English and Kannada, with planned expansion to Hindi, Tamil, and Telugu
Consent records are logged with timestamps, IP addresses, and the version of the consent notice displayed at the time of collection. Data principals may withdraw consent at any time through the account settings panel or by contacting the DPO.
5.2 Contractual Necessity
Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data principal is a party, or for taking steps at the request of the data principal prior to entering into a contract. This covers:
Provisioning of software subscriptions and user accounts
Processing payments and issuing invoices
Providing customer support and resolving technical issues
Onboarding employees and managing payroll
5.3 Legal Obligation
NovaSpark may process personal data as required to comply with applicable laws and regulations, including:
Tax and accounting obligations under the Income Tax Act, 1961, and GST laws
Regulatory reporting to the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) as applicable
Obligations under the Information Technology Act, 2000, and associated rules
Court orders, government directives, or regulatory notices
5.4 Legitimate Interests
Where permitted under applicable law, NovaSpark may process personal data based on legitimate business interests, provided such interests are not overridden by the rights and freedoms of the data principal. Legitimate interests invoked include:
Fraud detection, security monitoring, and abuse prevention
Internal analytics and service improvement (using pseudonymized data)
Business continuity and disaster recovery planning
Direct marketing to existing customers (subject to opt-out rights)

10. Rights of Data Principals
NovaSpark acknowledges and upholds the rights of data principals as defined under the DPDP Act 2023 and, for EU data subjects, under the GDPR. Data principals may exercise any of the following rights by submitting a request to privacy@novaspark.in or through the self-service portal at account.novaspark.in/privacy.

10.1 Right to Access
Data principals have the right to obtain confirmation of whether NovaSpark processes their personal data and, if so, to receive a copy of such data. We will provide a structured data export within 30 days of a verified request, free of charge for up to two requests per year.
10.2 Right to Correction and Updation
Data principals may request correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete data. NovaSpark will action verified correction requests within 15 business days.
10.3 Right to Erasure
Data principals may request deletion of their personal data where: (a) the purpose for which it was collected has been fulfilled; (b) consent has been withdrawn and no other lawful basis applies; or (c) the data has been unlawfully processed. NovaSpark will process erasure requests within 30 days, except where retention is required under law.
10.4 Right to Withdraw Consent
Where processing is based on consent, data principals may withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal. Following withdrawal, NovaSpark will cease processing within 7 business days.
10.5 Right to Grievance Redressal
Data principals may submit complaints or grievances to NovaSpark's Grievance Officer (see Section 14). If the complaint is not resolved within 30 days, data principals may escalate to the Data Protection Board of India (once constituted).
10.6 Right to Nominate (DPDP Act)
Data principals may nominate another individual to exercise data rights on their behalf in the event of death or incapacity. Nomination forms are available at account.novaspark.in/privacy.
10.7 GDPR-Specific Rights
For data principals located in the EU/EEA, the following additional rights apply:
Right to Data Portability (Article 20): Receive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format
Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18): Request restriction of processing during dispute or verification
Right to Object (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling
Rights related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22): Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions that produce significant effects
Right to Lodge a Complaint with a Supervisory Authority: EU data subjects may lodge complaints with their national data protection authority (e.g., the CNIL, BfDI, or ICO)

11. Data Security
NovaSpark implements a comprehensive, defense-in-depth security framework to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. Our Information Security Management System is aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and is subject to annual third-party audits.

11.1 Technical Safeguards
Encryption at rest using AES-256 for all databases containing personal data
Encryption in transit using TLS 1.2 and above for all data transmissions
Tokenization of payment card data; full card numbers are never stored on NovaSpark systems
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) enforced for all internal systems and customer-facing admin portals
Role-based access control (RBAC) ensuring staff access only the data necessary for their role
Vulnerability scanning and penetration testing conducted quarterly by accredited third parties
Web Application Firewall (WAF) and DDoS protection via AWS Shield Advanced
Automated intrusion detection and SIEM-based alerting (Splunk Enterprise Security)
11.2 Organizational Safeguards
Mandatory data protection training for all employees at onboarding and annually thereafter
Background verification for all staff with access to sensitive personal data
Clean desk and clear screen policy enforced across all offices
Non-disclosure agreements with all employees, contractors, and vendors
Privacy-by-design and privacy-by-default embedded in the software development lifecycle (SDLC)
Vendor security assessments conducted before onboarding and annually thereafter
11.3 Physical Safeguards
Biometric access control to server rooms and data processing areas
24/7 CCTV surveillance at all NovaSpark office premises
Visitor management system with mandatory NDA signing for all external visitors
Secure disposal of physical documents through cross-cut shredding

13. Children's Data
NovaSpark's commercial SaaS services are not directed at individuals under the age of 18 years. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. Our registration flows include a date-of-birth verification step that prevents accounts from being created by individuals under 18.

If NovaSpark becomes aware that personal data of a minor has been inadvertently collected, we will:
Immediately suspend the relevant account
Notify the parent or guardian if contact information is available
Delete all personal data associated with the minor within 48 hours
Conduct an internal review to identify and close the vulnerability that allowed the data to be collected

For NovaSpark's EdTech products (where applicable) that are specifically designed for use by students under 18, processing is conducted in compliance with Section 9 of the DPDP Act, which requires verifiable parental consent before processing children's personal data. These products additionally prohibit the use of children's data for behavioral tracking, targeted advertising, or profiling.
